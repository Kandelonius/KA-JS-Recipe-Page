Caprese Salad

  • Time: 30 min
  • serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
medium tomatoes 4 sliced
fresh basil leaves 1/4 c
fresh mozzarella 1/2 pound sliced
extra virgin olive oil 2 T
balsamic vinegar 2 T
ground mustard 1 t
salt 1/8 t
pepper 1/8 t

DirectionsArrange the tomatoes, cheese and basil on a serving platter. Whisk the vinaigrette ingredients; drizzle over salad and serve.

Spaghetti

  • Time: 2 hours
  • Serves: 8+
Ingredients Quantity
ground beef 1 pound
hot Italian sausage 1 pound
extra virgin olive oil 1/4 c
whole onion 1
garlic clove 4
lapsang soushong tea 2 T
red pepper flakes 1 T
carrot 3 medium
fire roasted tomatoes 3.5 pounds
fire roasted bell pepper 2 pounds with liquid
oregano 1 T
herbes de provence 1 T
corn 3 ears
spaghetti 2 pounds

Step 1: Place the beef and Italian sausage in a heavy bottom large sause pot with the olive oil on med/high heat until the meat has browned. Meanwhile (or in advance) cut the onion into a large dice and mince the garlic. Once the meat has browned add the onion, garlic, tea, and the red pepper flakes.
Step 2: Grate the carrot and cut the tomatoes and bell peppers and add them to the pot once the onion is translucent. Add the oregano and herbes de provence and reduce heat to medium/low. Let simmer for 1 hour.
Step 3: Remove the corn from the husks. After the hour has ellapsed increase stove temperature to medium, taste the sauce and add more of any of the above herbs you may want to add 'to taste' as well as the corn. Steadily add uncooked spaghetti to the sauce stirring carefully. Once all of the spaghetti is added allow to cook while stirring regularly for ten minutes (or however long the noodles are meant to cook) then give it a final taste, adjust and serve.

Focaccia

  • Time: 3 hours
  • Serves: 6 - 8
Ingredients Quantity
water 1.5 c
extra virgin olive oil 4 T
bread flour 4 c
salt 2 t
dry active yeast 2 t
rosemary 1 T
coarse salt 1 t

Step 1: Using a bread machine, create your basic dough:
Add the water and olive oil, then cover the liquid with flour. Add the salt (half each in two corners), then make a small well in the middle of the flour and add the yeast. Start the dough cycle, which will last for roughly 90 minutes. Add the rosemary as well as the coarse salt and hand knead dough to incorporate them.
Step 2:Coat an oval or square metal baking dish, roughly 9″ x 13″ and 2″-3″ deep liberally with olive oil. Take your dough and gently stretch it until it is roughly the shape of your pan, lay it in the pan, and push it into the corners to fit. Jiggle the pan back and forth to make sure the bottom of the dough is coated and slides smoothly. Cover and let the rest for an hour, or until it has risen by half.
Step 3: Push down an interesting pattern of indentations using your fingers, coat the top with yet more olive oil to fill the indentations, and bake in a moderately hot brick pizza oven, or on a pizza stone, (apx. 500°F.) If your fire is bright and your dome hot, you might want to wait for it to cool down a bit first. Depending on your oven temperature, your focaccia should cook for somewhere between 20 and 30 minutes.

Source: Forno-Bravo-Focaccia
